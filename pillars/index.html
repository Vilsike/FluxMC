Pillars of the Realm

Four foundations that keep Crownfall political, playable, and long-term.

Houses, not factions

Your identity is your House. Banner, territory, allies, and reputation. Houses can rise through many paths, not just combat.

Influence

Influence is realm-wide power. It reflects what you control, what you produce, and what you can rally. It ties directly into governance, status, and major outcomes.

Diplomacy and economy

Markets, taxes, trade routes, and agreements are real tools. Wealth and leverage matter because they enable projects, alliances, and war readiness.

War is opt-in

War is declared and scheduled. Conflicts are objective-based and designed to have lasting consequences. You can be powerful without constant raiding, but if you declare war, it should matter.
